Smart Sleep



The client had a renting apartment business that was about to grow from 10-15 apartments in Warsaw to around 200 in different Polish cities within one year. They faced the challenge of mantaining customer service quality, their main strength. We were asked to create an app that helped on this process and also gave extra value to their service.

We set a meeting to start a conversation with the client, then we made a list of facts and challenges about the project and ideated around possible features for the MVP together.



User research

In order to better understand what people like and expect from this kind of service we conducted several qualitative interviews to different users.



We found two descrete types of traveler.

For business people traveling its more like a rutine, they donâ€™t want to be worried by details on the booking. They look for straight forward experiences, and tend to go back to the same places if satisfied. "I don't want to waste time whenever I travel for work".

On the other hand, tourist declared to spend more time looking for the right fit. They appreciate more other faciliies like extra information or 24/7 customer attention. "I would appreciate suggestions about what to visit in the area"

There seemed to exist a generalisated feeling of uncerntity about what the apartment and its access going to be.



App features and sections

We did a short list of the main features the app would have and the basic architecture.

  • Booking section
    • Trips search
    • Last trips
    • You may like
  • Travel section
    • Travel guide
    • Tips for your stay
    • You may need
    • Currency details
    • Weather details
  • Stay section
    • Home guide
    • Services offer
    • Client support
    • Area suggestions


User flow

Based on that, we designed a user flow with four main screens, representing different times of the journey: booking, pack, travel and home. We added a fifth profile screen.



I was specially involved in the desgin of the flowmap.



Finally, we made visual guidelines for the UI, deciding style and colors.
